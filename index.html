Welcome to Mindful Mornings!

Are you stuck in bed snoozing too long? Are you rather the type to sip your coffee, scroll your mobile phone aimlessly and procrastinate only to rush through your morning routines? We have some tips on how to reclaim your mindful mornings and get a healthy start of your day!

Breathing exercise

Start your mindful day with a breathing exercise. You only need a minute to feel relaxed and grounded. Follow your breath as you breathe in and out, try to focus only on your breathing throughout your exercise. If your mind wanders, gently re-focus on your breathing. You can also place one hand on your stomage and the other one your chest to feel the subtle movements as you breathe. The cirle below gives you the beat to follow. 5 seconds in, 5 seconds out...

Eat well

Your body is your temple (large or small place of worship all the same). Eat a nutritious and balanced breakfast. When you indulge in your food mindfully, take the time to feel the flavours, temperature and texture of the food. Here are a few yummy recepies:

    Smoothie
  • 1 cup Pineapple
  • 2 cups Spinach
  • 1/2 cup Grapes
  • 1 1/2 cup Orange Juice
  • 1 Banana
  • Ice
    • Add all ingredients to a blender and blend until smooth and creamy.
      Overnight oats
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 3/4 Tbsp chia seeds
  • 2 Tbsp peanut butter
  • 1 Tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • Sliced banana, strawberries or raspberries
  • Flaxseed meal or additional chia seeds
    • To a small bowl with a lid, add almond milk, chia seeds, peanut butter, and maple syrup (or other sweetener) and stir with a spoon to combine. The peanut butter doesn't need to be completely mixed with the almond milk (doing so leaves swirls of peanut butter to enjoy the next day). Add oats and stir a few more times. Then press down with a spoon to ensure all oats have been moistened and are immersed in almond milk. Cover securely with a lid or seal and set in the refrigerator overnight (or for at least 6 hours) to set/soak. Overnight oats will keep best in the refrigerator 12-24 hours.

      Avocado egg sandwich
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 pinch salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp butter, softened
  • 2 slices Italian bread
  • 1 ounce Swiss cheese, sliced
  • 1 ounce Cheddar cheese, sliced
  • ½ ripe avocado, sliced
    • Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Crack eggs into skillet; cook for 1 to 2 minutes. Flip and cook egg until done, 2 to 5 minutes more. Transfer eggs to a plate and season with salt and pepper. Spread butter onto 1 side of each bread slice.Heat a skillet over medium heat. Lay bread slices in the hot skillet, butter-side down. Quickly layer Swiss cheese, 1 fried egg, Cheddar cheese, 1 fried egg, avocado, and remaining bread slice, butter-side up. Carefully flip sandwich to grill other side until golden brown, 3 to 4 minutes. Remove sandwich from skillet and cut in half.
      Baked oats
  • 2 cups oats
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 cup blueberries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup blueberries (fresh or frozen)
  • ½ cup chopped nuts & seeds
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup almond or coconut milk
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
    • In one bowl, mix together dry mixture of oats, nuts, seeds, baking powder, spices & add in melted coconut oil & blueberries. Another bowl, mix together egg, milk, maple syrup & whisk to combine. Pour oat mixture into a baking dish and pour wet mixture over top. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown
